How to fix slow internet

By Owen Ben Davies

Hello, so I've had quite a few requests from family members who are now doing a lot of video calls to help fix their slow internet. To help everyone, I thought I'd share my instructions with you all. Please feel free to share this site with everyone you know.

  1. Make your device is in good working order:
    1. Check you are connected to your WIFI, especially for tablets and phones
    2. Install the latest updates for your device
    3. Close other apps and browser windows as this will use up internet and slow your device down
    4. Find out if other people are using your internet. The more people using it at once the better internet you will need
    5. Don’t use the microwave, cordless phones or baby monitors at the same time as these use the same signal as WIFI and cause interference
  2. Run a speed test:
    1. Google "internet speed test"
    2. Click the blue "run speed test" button
  3. Check your upload and download rates:
    1. For video calls you need at least 2 Mbps download AND 2 Mbps upload
    2. For large group video calls you need 8 Mbps download AND 2 Mbps upload
    3. For SD films you need at least 3 Mbps download
    4. For HD films you need at least 5 Mbps download
    5. For 4K films you need at least 25 Mbps download
    6. This is what is needed per device, so multiply by the number of devices you have
  4. Check your latency, this is how much of a delay there is:
    1. Less than 10 ms is great
    2. 30 ms is good
    3. 100 ms or more will cause problems
  5. If you have other devices (laptops / phone / tablet):
    1. Perform step 2 on each of your devices
    2. If devices have different speeds it’s probably an issue with that device
  6. Find your router:
    1. The router is the small box provided by your broadband company connected to the phone line or the box probided by your cable TV provider
    2. Ensure it is not covered with anything that will block your WIFI signal
    3. Make sure none of the cables are loose, e.g. where it connnects to the socket
    4. Move your device right next to the router or if you can plug your device in with a cable
    5. Re-run the speed tests in step 2
  7. If the speed results are similar when you are right next to the router:
    1. It is probably an issue with your broadband
    2. If it is less than the speed you pay for then contact your internet provider and get them to resolve the issue.
  8. If the results are much quicker when you are right next to the router:
    1. It is probably an issue with the WIFI in your home
    2. Check there are no devices from step 1e causing interference
    3. Make sure nothing is blocking the signal as in step 6b
    4. If possible, be close to the router when doing video calls
    5. If you can, plug your device into your router with a cable, especially for a TV
    6. If your router is more than a few years old, contact your internet provider and see if they offer a newer model
    7. If you have a large house, an old house with thick walls, or a modern flat with metal stud walls then this might be stopping your wireless internet signal. It is possible to buy MESH WI-FI which will extend your signal. Some internet providers offer them, if not you will need to buy a system and set it up yourself.

Good luck, and I hope this helps you!
